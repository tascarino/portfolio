Projects and More

Light's Path: A Puzzle Game Demo

Light's Path is a video game demo made in the Godot application. The player moves around a beam of light using the mouse pointer. Using the physical player character, they can redirect the path of the beam by walking into and turning mirrors from a handle on the back.

A level is completed when the player successfully redirects the light to hit the large crystal, thus opening the door to process to the next level.

Various Ceramics Pieces

I've made a variety of ceramic works over the years. These works range from simple pinch pots to things like slab butter dishes, wheel thrown pottery, sculpture work, and a large hand-built vase.

Pieces use either earthenware or stoneware clay as well as a variety of glaze types: underglaze, commercially manufactured, or glazes that were made on campus in the ceramics studio.

Plethora of Pets

I've had a variety of pets over the course of my life. In total, I've had one dog, 2 hamsters, 12 birds (2 cockatiels, 10 budgies), a handful of fish, and a small frog. We briefly watched over my sisters two snakes, as well.

We've had the most birds out of everything, since two of our previous budgies had 5 babies, giving us a grand total of 9 birds at the same time. Right now, however, we only have two budgies: Pickle and Pepper.

Snowfall: A Hypothetical Game Project

A project I've had in mind for a while, after making the afformentioned game demo, was to try to make a fully-fleshed out game in my spare time. I want to work with one of my friends to adapt a D&D campaign I am a part of into a story-based game, with maybe a little combat. The game engine I will use is still undecided.

The game would take place in a small town called Snowfall, following four individuals in their misadventures navigating through a zombie apocalyse, while also discovering secrets about the town itself.
